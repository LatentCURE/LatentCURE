CURE-JEPA: Self-Supervised Data Curation with Predictive Video Priors for Robot Demonstrations

Anonymous RA-L Submission

Abstract

Robot imitation learning increasingly relies on large and diverse datasets, yet data quality remains a major bottleneck for downstream policy learning. Existing data curation methods typically rely on indirect proxies that are not fully aligned with whether demonstrated actions benefit task execution. This letter presents CURE-JEPA, a self-supervised data curation framework that formulates data quality in terms of how actions contribute to task execution, where high-quality actions should drive the task state toward a successful completion while providing smooth and reliable supervision for policy learning. Built on predictive video priors from V-JEPA 2, we model action-conditioned dynamics in latent space and evaluate demonstrations from two criteria: (1) a progress-deficiency criterion for coarse progress estimation and (2) a context-calibrated smoothness criterion for identifying fine-grained action adjustments required by the task. Simulation and real-world experiments show that CURE-JEPA improves average data quality by 21% and downstream policy performance by 14% over state-of-the-art baselines.
